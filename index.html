    How it works

    Adding meaningful moments.

    Mindscapes offers journaling within three categories: Highlights, Lowlights, and Free Writes (Rides). These entries will be added to a collective map which guides you in your personal growth and emotional journey.

    Explore your emotions and learn about yourself.

    Mindscapes is an application of Water Logic (de Bono, 1994), a procedure which translates the momentary consciousness to schematics of perceptions, which shows which thoughts are dominant, which thoughts lead to cyclical patterns.

    Find deeper meanings through long-term reflection about your thoughts.

    Mindscapes offers a cartographic view of the thoughts and expressions appreciate, learn from, and find peace with our past as we take steps toward the future ahead.

    Express yourself knowing your privacy is taken care of.

    Your account is only accessible by you and your data is protected by SSL in transit and your entries are encrypted at storage in our data base.

    Experience a holistic approach to your psycho-emotional wellbeing.

    During your end-of-month review, we encourage you to share where you stand on our six dimensions of wellness: Mind, Body, Soul, Work, Play, and Love. Over time, you can start to see which areas of your life are getting the most attention and which areas might need a little more love.

    Be part of the collective mental models through the forum.

    Mindscape forum is a space for sharing collective mental maps across users of the platforms. It gives the opportunity to see interconnectivity and interdependence of the world.

    Our Story

    Mindscapes is developed by Charlotte Qin, a mixed background designer who had worked through her own mental barriers and resistence to bring the project to light.

    During the early development of the project, she had received tremendeous encouragement from Jerome Michtell who was then a MA Painting student at the Royal College of Art. Jerome has taken the technique of Mindscapes to develop a workshop to a community of youth who were considered to be at rist, i.e. ready to join a gang, or have living in hostels or other forms of care. Through two sessions of workshop, it was clearly demonstrated that the process had helped the participants to see their goals in life and makes them believe in their abilities of creating a business that positively impcts the community.

    Charlotte had also received tremendeous help from her friends who are software engineers, data visualization, and interaction designers.
